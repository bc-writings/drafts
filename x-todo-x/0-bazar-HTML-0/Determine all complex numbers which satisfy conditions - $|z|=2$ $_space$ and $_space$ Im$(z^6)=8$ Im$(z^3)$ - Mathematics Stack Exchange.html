    Im(𝑧6)=Im(64e6𝑖𝜃)=64sin6𝜃,Im(8𝑧3)=Im(64e3𝑖𝜃)=64sin3𝜃Im⁡(z6)=Im⁡(64e6iθ)=64sin⁡6θ,Im⁡(8z3)=Im⁡(64e3iθ)=64sin⁡3θ

    Without loss of generality, we can reduce the equations to

    |𝑧|=1,Im(𝑧6)=Im(𝑧3)|z|=1,Im(z6)=Im(z3)

    From this, we can say that when 𝑧=𝜔𝑖z=ωi (where 𝜔𝑖ωi are the cube roots of unity), the equations will definitely be true.

    After that, use the polynomial expansions for 𝑧6z6 and 𝑧3z3 considering 𝑧=𝑥+𝑖𝑦z=x+iy which is effectively solving

    6𝑥𝑦5 −20𝑥3𝑦3+6𝑥5𝑦=(3𝑦𝑥2−𝑦3)6xy5 −20x3y3+6x5y=(3yx2−y3)
    upon condition that
    𝑥2+𝑦2=1x2+y2=1
     which is a unit circle.

    You can access the following graph here

    The intersections of the black graph with the red circle and the blue points with coordinates labelled are the solutions required.
