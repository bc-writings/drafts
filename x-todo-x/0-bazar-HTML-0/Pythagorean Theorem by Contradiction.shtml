Pythagorean Theorem by Contradiction

Problem

Proof

Let h be the altitude from the right angle. The right triangle (a,b,c) is split into two triangles, (x,h,a) and (h,y,b), both similar to the triangle (a,b,c). In particular, h2=xy.

Assume to the contrary that a2+b2≠c2. Let, e.g, a2+b2<c2. Then, by similarity, also

x2+h2<a2 and h2+y2<b2.

Summing up yields a contradiction:

a2+b2>(x2+h2)+(h2+y2)=x2+y2+2h2=x2+y2+2xy=(x+y)2=c2,

so that a2+b2>c2, contrary to our assumption.

Acknowledgment

The proof by K. B. SUBRAMANIAM, Math. Gaz. 102 (2018) p. 128 was kindly pointed to me by Grgoire Nicollier.
