e in the Pascal Triangle

Harlan Brothers has recently discovered the fundamental constant e hidden in the Pascal Triangle; this by taking products - instead of sums - of all elements in a row:

If sn is the product of the terms in the nth row, then

limn→∞sn−1sn+1s2n=e.

Proof

The terms in the nth row of the Pascal Triangle are the binomial coefficients (nk) so that

sn=∏k=0n(nk)=(n!)n+1∏nk=0k!2.

This gives

sn+1sn=(n+1)! n+2(n!)n+1⋅∏nk=0k!2∏n+1k=0k!2=(n+1)n+2n!1⋅1(n+1)!2=(n+1)n+1(n+1)!=(n+1)nn!.

It follows that

sn+1sn−1s2n=(n+1)nn!⋅(n−1)!nn−1=(n+1n)n=(1+1n)n.

Which is a well known expression whose limit is e.

References

  1. H. J. Brothers, Pascal's triangle: The hidden stor-e, The Mathematical Gazette, March 2012, 145
  2. H. J. Brothers, Finding e in Pascal's triangle, Mathematics Magazine, 85, No. 1 (2012), 51
